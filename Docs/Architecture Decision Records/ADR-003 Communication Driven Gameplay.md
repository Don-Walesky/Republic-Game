# ADR-003: Communication Driven Gameplay

## Problem

How do we make political information feel like it comes from *people*, not a UI?

## Context

Too many political games feel like spreadsheet simulators. Information appears in lists. Stats update mysteriously.

We wanted information to feel *earned through communication*.

## Decision

**Information comes from NPCs through communication.**

- Phone calls deliver urgent news
- Visitors bring proposals
- Emails contain reports
- Cabinet meetings present options
- Ministers advise on their specialties

## Why

1. **Immersion**: Information feels real
2. **Role-play**: You're getting briefed, not reading stats
3. **Pacing**: Communication controls information flow
4. **Consequence**: You can ignore advisors (at your peril)
5. **Personality**: Different advisors have different styles

## Trade-offs

✅ Much better immersion
✅ Natural information pacing
❌ More NPC voice acting needed
❌ Dialog system complexity
❌ Can't just "look up" information

## Consequences

- Every system needs NPC representatives
- Ministers become actual characters
- Players form relationships with advisors
- Dismissing advisors creates consequences
- Information can be strategically withheld (if advisor dislikes you)
